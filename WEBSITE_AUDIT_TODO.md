# Website Audit

## Executive Summary

- Overall score: 55/100
- Biggest strengths: Clean visual system, clear service categories, bilingual support, consistent spacing, and a polished premium feel for a young agency site.
- Biggest weaknesses: No real conversion system, no substantive case studies, no testimonials or client proof, weak SEO metadata, placeholder portfolio links, and missing legal/operational pages.
- Highest-priority improvements: Replace placeholder links with real destinations, add a proper lead capture flow, strengthen proof and credibility, expand the portfolio into real case studies, and fix SEO/accessibility basics.
- Limitation: This is currently a single-page site. I could audit the home page and shared layout components, but there were no separate case study, pricing, blog, legal, or contact pages to inspect beyond the home-page sections.

---

# Critical Issues

Items that should be fixed immediately.

- [ ] Replace all placeholder project links (`href="#"`) with real case study pages or external project URLs.
  Why it matters: Clicking a project card currently goes nowhere, which damages credibility and makes the portfolio feel unfinished.
  Recommendation: Create dedicated case study pages for each project or link to live demos and ensure every card has a real destination.
  Estimated impact (High/Medium/Low): High

- [ ] Add a real contact form instead of relying only on email and WhatsApp links.
  Why it matters: Email-only CTAs create friction, lose leads, and prevent qualification of budget, timeline, and project type.
  Recommendation: Add a short conversion-focused form with name, email, company, budget, service need, and project brief, plus a success state and clear response-time promise.
  Estimated impact (High/Medium/Low): High

- [ ] Replace the generic page title and weak meta description with SEO-focused, market-specific metadata.
  Why it matters: The current title is brand-only and the description is thin, so search engines and social previews do not communicate value or intent.
  Recommendation: Use a keyword-relevant title, a stronger meta description, Open Graph tags, Twitter tags, and localized metadata for each language.
  Estimated impact (High/Medium/Low): High

- [ ] Add proof-based trust assets immediately: testimonials, client logos, and measurable outcomes.
  Why it matters: The site claims expertise, but there is almost no external validation to reduce buyer skepticism.
  Recommendation: Add testimonials with names and roles, a client logo strip, and project metrics such as conversion lift, speed improvements, leads generated, or completion timelines.
  Estimated impact (High/Medium/Low): High

- [ ] Fix the accessibility of the FAQ and other interactive controls.
  Why it matters: The FAQ buttons remove visible focus styling and lack robust ARIA relationships, which makes keyboard navigation and screen reader use weaker than it should be.
  Recommendation: Add accessible focus states, `aria-controls`, unique IDs, and ensure all interactive elements have visible, consistent keyboard focus indicators.
  Estimated impact (High/Medium/Low): High

  Suggested content: Starting prices, package ranges, what is included, and custom-project notes.

- [ ] About
  Why it should exist: The current section is too short to establish a real story or founder credibility.
  Suggested content: Founder bio, mission, values, team photos, and why the studio exists.

- [X] Contact
  Why it should exist: A dedicated contact page reduces friction and improves lead generation.
  Suggested content: Form, email, phone, location, business hours, and response expectations.

- [ ] Testimonials
  Why it should exist: Social proof is currently missing.
  Suggested content: Client quotes, role/company, project context, and outcomes.

- [ ] Industries
  Why it should exist: Niching improves clarity and SEO.
  Suggested content: Target industries, pain points, and tailored service examples.

- [ ] Tech Stack
  Why it should exist: Technical buyers want implementation confidence.
  Suggested content: Frontend, backend, CMS, automation, analytics, and hosting stack.

- [ ] Blog
  Why it should exist: It is one of the best ways to build authority and organic traffic.
  Suggested content: Thought leadership, comparisons, case studies, and educational SEO content.

- [ ] Resources
  Why it should exist: Helpful for lead capture and authority building.
  Suggested content: Guides, checklists, downloadable assets, and templates.

- [ ] Free Audit
  Why it should exist: It is a strong low-friction lead magnet for agency buyers.
  Suggested content: Offer details, sample audit outputs, and booking CTA.

- [ ] Careers
  Why it should exist: Even small agencies benefit from a credibility page when hiring or growing.
  Suggested content: Open roles, culture, values, and application CTA.

- [ ] Privacy Policy
  Why it should exist: Required for trust and basic compliance.

- [ ] Terms
  Why it should exist: Protects both the business and clients.

- [ ] Cookie Policy
  Why it should exist: Needed if analytics or marketing tools are used.
  Suggested content: Cookie categories, consent details, and opt-out guidance.
